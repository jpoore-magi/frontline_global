Why Travel Insurance Matters

Every year, thousands of travelers face trip cancellations, medical emergencies abroad, theft, lost baggage, and unforeseen delays—often at high out-of-pocket cost. Travel insurance acts as a safety net, providing reimbursement and support when the unexpected occurs.

  • Trip Protection: Cover non-refundable travel costs if your trip is canceled or interrupted.
  • Medical Coverage: Ensure access to hospital care, evacuation, and repatriation overseas.
  • 24/7 Global Assistance: Get support anywhere in the world—from flight rebooking to emergency logistics.

FAQs About Travel Insurance

Is travel insurance worth it?
Yes—typically 5–7% of trip cost guards against financial losses from medical emergencies or cancellations.
Can I get coverage last minute?
Absolutely—policies are often available even 24 hours before departure.
Will a credit card travel plan suffice?
Usually not—most credit cards offer limited secondary coverage, while formal insurance provides primary, comprehensive protection.
